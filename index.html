I am a researcher on forest, climate, and carbon

My research interests lie in the intersection of forest ecology and atmosphere science.

I am an earth system scientist who focuses on the mechanism of land-atmosphere coupling in a changing climate. Specifically, I am interested in the impacts, drivers, and mechanisms of forest disturbance regimes with increasing extreme wind events across space and time using multi-spectral and LiDAR remote sensing images, statistical and machine learning approaches, and earth system models.

My research goal is to bridge basic science in climate, carbon cycle, and ecosystem dynamics and actions in nature-based climate solutions.

Years of research experience in climate change impacts have expanded my research interests to actionable climate solutions. During my postdoc at Carnegie Institute for Science, I have worked on assessing the potential of bioenergy with carbon capture and storage using waste biomass. At MIT, I am working on nature-based solutions.

Beyond the academic realm, I am an avid learner and practitioner of permaculture. I take pride in cultivating a backyard garden for over three years. I cherish the way of zero-waste living and am dedicatedly working towards fully embracing this sustainable path.
