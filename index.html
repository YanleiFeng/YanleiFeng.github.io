Yanlei Feng

Postdoc Researcher on climate impact science and climate solutions

Research interests lie in the intersection of forest ecology and atmosphere science.

As a researcher in earth science, I am deeply concerned about the warming climate and its impacts on earth, especially terrestrial ecosystem. My Ph.D. research focuses on how forests respond to a changing environment with increasing extreme storms. This is crucial for understanding the observed increase in tree mortality in tropics and a weakening of the carbon sink globally.

Years of research experience in climate change impacts have expanded my research interests to actionable climate solutions. During my postdoc at Carnegie Institute for Science, I have worked on bioenergy with carbon capture and storage. At MIT, I am working on nature-based solutions. I am also an active learner and practitioner in permaculture and have grown backyard gardens for 3 years.
