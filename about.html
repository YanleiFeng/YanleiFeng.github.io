My academic journey began with a focus on Geography and Environmental Science at the University of Waterloo, where I gained geospatial analysis skills in GIS and remote sensing. My undergraduate thesis on deforestation in Paragominas, Amazonia planted the seed for my PhD academic career in tropical forest ecology. My graduate study at Cornell University prepared me well with initial research experience and built my coding skills. These experiences led to a wonderful 5-year Ph.D. experience at UC Berkeley, where I studied tropical cyclones, convective storms, forest ecology, earth system modeling, and statistical and machine learning, etc.
